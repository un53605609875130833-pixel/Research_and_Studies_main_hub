01 // Overview

What the division does

Research & Studies researches, documents, and studies the unknown on behalf of the Vlandask Research Corporation. The division is organised into four departments, each pursuing a distinct line of inquiry.

To conduct research, personnel must first join a department. Department membership requires Junior Researcher rank or above, and personnel may not belong to more than one department at a time.

04 // Regulations

Testing & tool regulations

Every test conducted under the division's name is bound by these rules, regardless of who hosts it or which department it falls under.

4.1

Testing regulations

  1. Testing shall not exceed 30 minutes from the start of rally to the termination or return of the test subject to the test subject containment zone.
  2. Testing must at all times be accompanied by 1 SD per 2 Test Subjects (1 RCU per 2 CIS). Once entering the CTZ, testing must be accompanied by at least 1 RCU.
  3. The associated test must have a clear and depicted theory it is attempting to prove or disprove. Do not waste test subjects by testing with the pure intent to kill.
  4. All testing must be logged through official documentation in the associated logging channels. Take notes during the test and file paperwork once it concludes.
  5. You may order the termination of non-cooperative test subjects, or those attempting to escape the convoy or escort. You hold authority over your guards but may not order them to break their own guidelines.
  6. All CIS (Contained Infected Specimen) testing requires Recontainment Unit or Juggernaut Unit personnel as guards — 1 JU per 3 CIS, or 1 RCU per 2 CIS.
4.2

Tool regulations

  1. All tools shall be used within reason and with justifiable cause. Do not tag an escaping test subject.
  2. The Tag tool is only to be used on CIS or TS personnel being actively tested. It grants immunity to turrets, but has a maximum range — if the subject leaves that range they are no longer tagged and are considered breached, subject to termination.

Fires a beam at the subject, mutating, damaging, killing, or atomically deconstructing them. Sometimes lethal.

Radiation Chamber

Radiates subjects with infectious radiation that can spread to non-immune personnel. 100% lethal.

RestrictionAssistant Researcher+ use freely. Lower ranks must be accompanied by Senior Researcher+.
OperationSwipe Door Lock keypad → close door → set frequency → close blinds → press red button to fire.
MutationsSubjects progressively turn green with each blast; additional heads may grow with repeated exposure.
Frequency 4–5F4 is a weaker version of F5. F5 boosts the speed of infected subjects. F1–3 remain undocumented.
Never have the door or blinds open while firing. Do not approach irradiated subjects without immunity — use an immune combative escort, and terminate infected subjects after testing to prevent spread.

Spins subjects at high speed. Never lethal. Anyone may operate it.

08 // Procedure

Entity hybrids

Hybrids are produced by combining the properties of two entities through controlled exposure.

To create a hybrid, collect at least 2 samples using empty vials from the RSD Specialist HQ, then give both to a willing test subject for consumption. The first vial dictates most abilities of the resulting hybrid; the second contributes minor secondary abilities.
All hybrid testing is restricted to Advisory Board+, Ontology members, and SRW members. Lower ranks must have experiments approved by Scientific Board+ and be accompanied by an Advisory Board+, Ontology, or SRW member.

10 // Getting Started

New member guide

Answers to the questions every new member asks first.

Step 1 — Read the regulations. Before anything else, read the Testing Regulations, Tool Regulations, and General Provision above. You are expected to know these; ignorance is not a valid defence.

Step 2 — Know your rank. You start as an Intern. Your first responsibility is to complete one orientation hosted by a Senior Researcher or above.

Step 3 — Complete your orientation. You cannot deploy freely or participate in tests until you've completed orientation and been promoted to Research Associate.

Step 4 — Join a department. Once you reach Junior Researcher, you become eligible to join Biology, Pathology, Experimental Engineering, or SRW. You cannot be in more than one at a time.

Take notes during the test — don't rely on memory. Afterward, file your log in the official documentation channel with: researcher name & rank, date & time, subject designation, risk level, purpose/hypothesis, procedure, personnel present, observations, outcome, and post-test notes. A test without a log is a regulation violation.

Promotions are not automatic. Meet the requirements listed for your rank above, build a strong documentation record (quality over quantity), conduct yourself professionally, and meet the activity thresholds genuinely. Promotions are reviewed and approved by the relevant authority — you cannot promote yourself, and asking repeatedly will not speed up the process.

Permissions scale directly with rank. Interns cannot deploy freely and may only assist. Research Associates may deploy freely but cannot host experiments. From Assistant Researcher upward, personnel may host tests and request experiments. Refer to the Ranks & Clearance table above for your specific tier.

Yes — every risk level has a minimum guard requirement, from 1 RCU at Low Risk up to all available security at Extreme Risk. See the Risk Levels table in the Testing section above for the exact breakdown by tier.

General questions — any Researcher or above. Orientation — a Senior Researcher+. Rank/promotion questions — Senior or Lead Researcher. Joining a department — that department's Head. Regulation violations or disputes — Scientific Board or Executive Command.

New personnel receive the standard field tool set — Radio, Cuffs, Wand, Tag, ID Paper, and Clipboard — along with the base R&S uniform. Higher access cards (Level 2 and above) are issued automatically as you're promoted; you do not need to request them separately. Report a missing or malfunctioning tool to your supervising Senior Researcher.

Each department maintains its own Trello board (linked in the Division Network section above). After a test, create a card under the appropriate list with your log — researcher name & rank, date & time, subject designation, risk level, hypothesis, procedure, and outcome. Attach any relevant screenshots as card attachments. Cards are reviewed by your Department Head or Scientific Board as part of promotion eligibility, so treat them as your permanent research record.

11 // Application

Entry Exam

Before applying to join Research & Studies, review the minimum eligibility requirements below. Applications from members who do not meet these requirements may be denied without further review.

§

Minimum eligibility requirements

  • Must have been a member of the Vlandask Research Corporation for a minimum of 7 days.
  • Must not be currently serving under disciplinary action, suspension, or probation.
  • Must demonstrate maturity and professionalism.
  • Must be capable of following orders and maintaining chain of command.
  • Must be able to communicate effectively with personnel from all divisions.
  • Must possess a basic understanding of procedures.
  • Must understand containment, security, and safety expectations.
  • Must be capable of producing professional written responses and reports.
Failure to meet these requirements may result in immediate denial of the application.
